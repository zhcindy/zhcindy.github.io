Pitch Discrimination Experiment

I designed a circuit with a team of 3 other students and used it to test our peers' ability to match pitch from memory. The key circuit elements include an nScope device, 555 timer chip, an operational amplifier, and a potentiometer. The circuit is paired in conjunction with a MATLAB program and outputs a tone at a given frequency. Read more about our methods and results here!

Skeeball Mechanics

I focused on mathematically modeling the motion of a ball rolled by an arm as a final project for a dynamics course. The mechanic equations of motion were derived and visualized in an animation using Mathematica.

The following factors were included in the derivations:
  • triple pendulum model for the throwing arm
  • Euler-Lagrange equations of motion with external forces for air drag
  • conserved Hamiltonian and additional external forces for impacts with the ground

For more details, please take a look at the final report.

Below: The derived equations of motion animated with Mathematica. The animation runs forward and backwards with respect to t.

Figure Skating Jumps

I investigated the biomechanics in figure skating jumps (the axel in particular) with a classmate using the video tracking software, Tracker. We compared jump parameters such as countermovement depth, take off velocity, and jump height between jumps of different number of rotations. Additionally, we explored angular velocity and total energy to understand better any relationships between number of rotations of a jump and its jump parameters. Read more about the project here.
