Injection Molded Toy Design

A 4 person, 10 week project that created an injection molded part based off a 4th grader's sketch of an imagined rocketship toy.

    My Role

  • Main CAD modeler for part and mold
  • Contributed to conceptual design
  • Contributed to CNC programming

The Starting Point: A 4th Grade Sketch

Our inspiration came from this sketch by a student at a local elementary school. The 4th grade class was involved in a tech outreach program in connection with Northwestern University and after learning how toys are made, the students were told to design their own, with the potential for it to be brought to life.

Part Design: Identical Halves + Nose Cone

For a simpler design, our rocketship body was composed of 2 identical halves secured together with a nose cone.

Rocketship fuselage design, 2 identical halves would snap together. (Sketched by a fellow team member).

Nose cone design, initially would be two halves (right) but later changed to one single part (left). (Sketched by a fellow team member).

Modeling: Part and Mold

Much of my contribution went into creating the models for both the parts, and then the mold. The CAD software used was Siemens NX (UG). Factors such as interferance fits, moldability were considered for the part, and material flow, shrinkage, and gates were considered for the mold.
