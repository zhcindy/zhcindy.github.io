Injection Molded Toy Design

A 4-person, 10-week project that created the part model, mold, and production run of an injection molded toy.

    My Role

  • Modeled (using NX/UG) part and mold for CNC machine programming
  • Programmed CNC operations to manufacture core and cavity molds
  • Injection molded batch of 75 plastic parts

The Starting Point: A 4th Grade Sketch

Our inspiration came from this sketch by a student at a local elementary school. The 4th grade class was involved in a tech outreach program in connection with Northwestern University. After learning how toys are made, the students were told to design their own, with the potential for it to be brought to life.

Part Design: Identical Halves + Nose Cone

For a simpler design, our rocketship body was composed of 2 identical halves secured together with a nose cone. (Sketches by a fellow team member).

rocketship fuselage design, 2 identical halves snap together

nose cone design, initially two halves (right) but later changed to one single part (left)

Modeling: Part and Mold

I modeled the part and mold using Siemens NX (UG). We considered factors such as interferance fits and moldability (on the part), and material flow, shrinkage, and gating locations (on the mold).

Machining and Injection Molding

We programmed CNC g-code for the cavity and core molds using NX to be machined by a Haas CNC machine. We post-processed the molds for tool marks and used them on the injection molding machine for a small production run of 75 parts. Each cycle produced one full assembly.

Molded Parts and Takeaways

Multiple common injection molding issues occurred, such as flash and bowing. We adjusted injection molding parameters such as cycle time and injection pressure to yield the best possible result. One of the main improvements for future iterations would be to decrease the part thickness for better material flow and to address the bowing issue.
