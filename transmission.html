Transmission Design

A 3-person, 4-week project which designed the shafts, gears and
bearing components for a gearbox with a target input torque.

    My Role

  • Responsible for one shaft and gear of the 3 shaft system
  • Determined gear and shaft geometry through stress calculations
  • Selected the proper bearing for mechanical performance of shaft

The Starting Point: Design Challenge

The goal is to design a two-member bracket with specific constraints to fail under a certain applied load. The load is applied at a predetermined location and the type of failure mode (tension, compression, buckling, excessive deformation) must also be predicted.

Conceptual Design: Failure Mode Calculations

To eliminate bending moments, we designed the bracket to contain only two-force members by attaching the two free endpoints to coincide with the loading point. We determined the bracket details (such as material thickness and a U-shaped cross section) through iterations of calculations to insure a tensile failure.
