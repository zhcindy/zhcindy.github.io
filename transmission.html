Transmission Design

A 3-person, 4-week project which designed the shafts, gears and bearing components for a gearbox with a target input torque.

    My Role

  • Oversaw the complete design of one shaft and gear of the 3 shaft system
  • Determined gear and shaft geometry through stress calculations
  • Selected the proper bearing for mechanical performance of shaft

The Starting Point: Challenge and Concept

The goal was to design a three-shaft gearing system that will take an input power rating and produce a specific output shaft speed. Our team designed the system to be compact in additional to the basic requirements, where shaft A and C were inline and half the length of shaft B. I worked on input shaft A, which included a shaft with a keyway, gear, and selection of a corresponding pair of support bearings.

Mechanical Design: Stress Analysis, Gear Forces, and Bearing Selection

I designed the shaft and gear geometry with consideration of the following factors:

  • shaft moments and reaction forces
  • principle stresses at critical cross sections of shaft
  • dynamic von-misses stress on shaft to account for variable bending and shear stresses
  • factor of safety for overall material strength of shaft and gear
  • gear forces and ratio with collaboration with adjacent gear system
